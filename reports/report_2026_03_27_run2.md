## Key Findings

### 1. 🚨 The System Cannot Execute Trades — Structural Staleness Block

This is a **showstopper bug**. The full pipeline (Scout ~5s + Analyst ~7s + Trader ~29s ≈ 41s minimum) always exceeds the 30s NORMAL staleness threshold. The system will **never** execute a trade in NORMAL regime under current configuration.

The `config.yaml` overrides the code default of 90s to 30s for NORMAL. This was likely set during an earlier phase when the pipeline was faster or when Trader used a faster model. With Opus as the Trader model, 30s is unreachable.

### 2. SUI Was the Big Miss — Two Correct Signals, Zero Execution

SUIUSDT SHORT was identified correctly **twice** (confluence 8 and 7, Trader confidence 0.68 and 0.62). SUI proceeded to drop 10.3% — the largest post-session move of any symbol. At 3x leverage, either trade would have returned 2.5–31% depending on exit strategy. Both expired by staleness (5.8s and 1.4s over threshold).

The TAOUSDT SHORT signal had a correct short-term read (price did decline to $323.90 from $326.10 during the session), but TAO was in the midst of a +17.7% squeeze fueled by Jensen Huang's NVIDIA endorsement and 113% monthly gains. The tight TP at $323.70 might have saved the trade, but the tail risk was extreme. The staleness expiration was accidentally beneficial.

### 4. Analyst Confluence Scoring Is Inconsistent at the Boundary

The Analyst rejected 5 of 7 confluence-7 decisions as "borderline" while accepting all 5 confluence-8 decisions. This creates an **undocumented effective threshold** that depends on the composition of the score (algorithmic base vs discretionary). The Analyst is applying qualitative judgment that may be correct but makes the system unpredictable.

### 5. Most Symbols Bounced — Session Timing Was Unfavorable

The session started during the post-selloff exhaustion phase. By 12:03 UTC, most symbols were near intraday lows with RSI in oversold territory. The market broadly bounced afterwards. The Trader's "oversold bounce risk" concern was validated for 7 of 9 symbols — only SUI continued lower.

DOGEUSDT was flagged INTERESTING 8 times (40% of evaluations) but produced zero valid Analyst setups. Its high raw volume creates frequent Criterion 4 (volume spike) triggers that don't translate to tradeable setups. The Scout needs a symbol-specific volume normalization or a higher spike threshold for high-volume assets.

## Recommendations

### P0 — Fix Staleness Threshold (Critical — System Cannot Trade)

**Problem**: The `config.yaml` NORMAL staleness threshold is 30,000ms, but the minimum pipeline time is ~35–40s. No trades can ever execute.

**Options** (pick one):
1. **Increase NORMAL threshold to 90s** (code default) — allows current pipeline to work but accepts staler data
2. **Increase to 60s** — compromise that allows most trades through (5 of 5 expired trades had age < 60s)
3. **Measure staleness from Trader start** instead of pipeline start — the Trader evaluates fresh data in its context, so the decision isn't truly 58s old; only the _candle_ is stale
4. **Add a "pipeline-adjusted" threshold**: `staleness = age_ms - (scout_ms + analyst_ms)` — subtracts known processing time

**Recommended**: Option 2 (60s) as an immediate fix, with Option 3 as a follow-up architectural change. At 60s, 4 of 5 trades would have passed; only the TAOUSDT (58.3s age) would have passed and one (TAOUSDT at 58.3s) would have squeaked through.

### P1 — Reduce Trader Latency

**Problem**: Trader (Opus) p50=29s, max=48s. This is the primary contributor to staleness.

**Options**:
- **Switch Trader to Sonnet** — would reduce to ~7–10s, making the full pipeline 19–24s (under 30s threshold)
- **Pre-compute entry plan in the Spine** — give the Trader a pre-built `{entry, SL, TP, R:R}` to accept/reject, reducing reasoning burden
- **Add a Trader timeout of 20s** with auto-WAIT fallback
- **Simplify the Trader prompt** — the current reasoning is excellent but verbose; a structured template could cut response time

**Recommended**: Switch to Sonnet and test if reasoning quality degrades meaningfully. The Trader's WAIT decisions were correct, but the SHORT signals that expired were also correct — so the Trader is already accurate enough that Sonnet may suffice.

### P2 — Fix Confluence 7 Inconsistency

**Problem**: Analyst rejects 71% (5/7) of confluence-7 decisions as "borderline" based on qualitative judgment about score composition, while accepting 100% of confluence-8 decisions.

**Options**:
1. **Make the threshold explicit in the prompt**: "If total confluence ≥ 7, set setup_valid=True regardless of score composition"
2. **Raise the threshold to 8** — matches actual behavior and reduces ambiguity
3. **Add algorithmic base minimum**: "Require algorithmic_base ≥ 5 AND total ≥ 7"

**Recommended**: Option 1 — enforce the documented threshold. The Analyst's qualitative concern is valid, but it should be captured in the confidence score, not in a binary valid/invalid override.

### P3 — Normalize DOGE Volume for Scout

**Problem**: DOGEUSDT triggers 8 INTERESTING verdicts (40%) but zero valid setups. Its naturally high volume causes false Criterion 4 triggers.

**Fix**: Use percentile-based volume spike detection instead of absolute ratio. Or apply a symbol-specific multiplier: DOGE volume_ratio threshold = 3.0x instead of 2.0x.

### P4 — Reduce Scout Latency

**Problem**: Scout p50=5.5s (target <2s) — adds 3.5s of unnecessary pipeline time to every evaluation.

**Options**:
- Verify OpenRouter is routing to the cheapest/fastest tier
